🎓 Your Story. Your Journey. Your Mastery.

🧩 Multimedia-Based Learning × Bloom's Taxonomy — The goal is simple: write an individual, relatable story that takes you from simple to complex — sorting ideas into a journey where every medium (images 🖼️, scripts 📝, audio 🎙️, components 🧩) becomes a stepping stone toward mastery. Each level of Bloom's Taxonomy is a chapter in your personal learning narrative.

📖 You are the protagonist. AI is your co-writer. Canva is your stage. The five levels below are the acts of your story — each one deeper, each one closer to owning the knowledge.

🧠

Act 1 · Remember — recall facts & concepts

📖 Your story begins with a question. "How does machine learning work?" You feed the topic to the AI. It replies with 🖼️ images and 📊 infographics — visual anchors that encode the idea into your memory. 📋 The storyboard serves as a background image — a single panoramic canvas that shows the overall picture before you dive into details. You don't just read about it — you see the whole journey at a glance.

🎯 Simple → raw topic becomes a visual anchor. The storyboard shows the big picture. You remember because you saw it.

📖

Act 2 · Understand — explain ideas in your own words

📖 Seeing isn't enough — you need a story. The AI transforms the visuals into a 📝 script and a 🎙️ voiceover. A narrative unfolds. You read the script. You hear the narration. The concept clicks — you can now explain it in your own words.

📈 Growing → visuals become narrative. You can now explain the idea.

🔍

Act 3 · Analyze — break information into parts

📖 Understanding isn't enough — now you diverge. The script is a whole. You break it into beats — branching out into many directions. For each beat you decide: should this be a 🎨 background? A 🗨️ speech-bubble? An 📊 infographic? 📝 Captions guide every choice — each caption tells you what the component should say, making placement decisions clear and intentional. 🧩 Typed components emerge — every one a fragment of meaning. You are now exploring structure in all directions. The goal isn't perfection — it's to diverge widely so you can later converge on what matters.

🧩 Diverge → captions light the way. Each component finds its place. More pieces = deeper understanding.

⚖️

Act 4 · Evaluate — diverge → converge, judge & refine

📖 After diverging — now you converge. The AI has no opinion here — this is your stage. 🎙️ You listen to the AI voiceover first — hearing the script read aloud reveals what visually needs to be there at each moment. 📝 Captions from the analyze stage guide component placement on the 🎬 Canva video timeline. The timings won't be perfect — that's the point. It is not meant to be perfect. Every rough arrangement teaches you something. You judge order, pacing, and impact — converging from chaos toward clarity. The goal is improved understanding, not a flawless timeline.

⚖️ Converge → listen first, then place. Captions + voiceover reveal what truly belongs.

🎨

Act 5 · Create — collaborative multimedia learning event

📖 The final act. Peak of the pyramid. 🎙️ The AI voiceover goes in first — it's fast, rough, and gets the timing skeleton in place so you start quickly. Then the human voiceover replaces it — placing narration in sync with your understanding, at the pace you actually need. 👥 We create this with students live in sessions — a true collaborative multimedia learning event. 🎵 Music and 🔊 SFX layer in. Together we polish every part that needs fixing — transitions, captions, audio levels — until the animation flows. This didn't exist before. Now you communicate it as a service — the finished animation becomes a shareable, teachable artifact. The story is yours. The knowledge is yours. And it was built together.

👥 Collaborate → AI voiceover starts fast, human voiceover syncs with understanding. Built live with students — shipped as a learning service.
